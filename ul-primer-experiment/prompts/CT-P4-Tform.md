Consider a system of N particles with Hamiltonian H(p,q) in thermal contact with a reservoir at temperature T. The canonical partition function is Z(T,V,N) = (1/N!h^{3N}) ∫ e^{−βH(p,q)} d³ᴺp d³ᴺq, where β = 1/k_BT. The Helmholtz free energy F = −k_BT ln Z connects to thermodynamic potentials via the Legendre transform: G = F + PV, H = F + TS.

The entropy S = −k_B ∑_i p_i ln p_i in the microcanonical ensemble, where p_i = 1/Ω(E) for all accessible microstates. The second law requires dS ≥ δQ/T, with equality for reversible processes. For an ideal gas, S(T,V,N) = Nk_B[ln(V/N) + (3/2)ln(2πmk_BT/h²) + 5/2] is the Sackur-Tetrode equation.

The fluctuation-dissipation theorem relates response to equilibrium fluctuations: χ(ω) = (1/k_BT) ∫₀^∞ ⟨A(t)A(0)⟩ e^{iωt} dt. Near a continuous phase transition at T_c, the correlation length diverges as ξ ~ |T−T_c|^{−ν} and the order parameter vanishes as φ ~ |T−T_c|^β.

The Ising model H = −J∑_{⟨ij⟩} s_is_j − h∑_i s_i has exact solution in 2D (Onsager): T_c = 2J/k_B ln(1+√2), with critical exponents β=1/8, ν=1, γ=7/4 characterizing universal scaling behavior. The renormalization group transformation R maps a Hamiltonian H to a coarse-grained H' = R(H) by integrating out short-wavelength modes. Fixed points H* = R(H*) correspond to universality classes, and critical exponents are determined by the eigenvalues of the linearized RG flow at the fixed point: λ_i = b^{y_i} where b is the rescaling factor.
