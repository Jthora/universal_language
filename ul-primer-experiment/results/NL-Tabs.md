

**Level 1 — Physical Chemistry**
Cellulose and lignin undergo exothermic oxidation when their temperature exceeds ~300 °C (flash point). The released energy radiates and convects to adjacent lignocellulosic material, raising it past the same threshold, thereby sustaining a chain of combustion reactions across contiguous fuel.

**Level 2 — Thermodynamics / Heat Transfer**
A localized energy release raises the temperature of nearby fuel above its ignition threshold. Because the energy output of the reaction exceeds the activation energy needed to ignite the next unit of fuel, the process is self-propagating through any continuous medium of sufficient fuel density.

**Level 3 — Systems Dynamics**
A positive feedback loop drives the spread: the process's output (heat) is also the input required to trigger the same process in neighboring elements. As long as susceptible elements are within the effective range of the output, the cascade continues.

**Level 4 — Network / Contagion Theory**
An activated node converts its neighbors by transmitting the very condition that caused its own activation. Propagation persists whenever connected, susceptible nodes exist within transmission range — the same structure underlying epidemics, information cascades, and chain reactions.

**Level 5 — Abstract Principle**
*A process that produces the conditions for its own initiation will propagate through any medium that satisfies those conditions.*
