

## From Intuition to Formal Object: Stages of Concept Crystallization

The formation of a scientific concept like "gene" follows a trajectory that the formalism above actually models quite suggestively — if we read Ω(σ,τ) as a *meaning field* distributed over a conceptual space σ and evolving in time τ. Here are the stages and the dynamics driving transitions between them:

---

### Stage 1 — Diffuse Pre-Conceptual Intuition

A concept begins as a broad, low-amplitude distribution across conceptual space: Ω(σ,τ₀) is spread wide, lacking localization. Mendel observed regularities in inheritance but had no word or sharp referent. At this stage, the field is governed primarily by the **diffusion term** (κ²/2ρ)D²σΩ — meaning spreads, blurs, and overlaps with neighboring ideas. There is no self-reinforcing structure yet. The concept is essentially a fluctuation above noise.

**What drives departure from this stage:** The *nonlinear self-interaction* g_loc|Ω|²Ω. Once a region of conceptual space accumulates enough density of attention, use, and explanatory success, self-reinforcement kicks in — usage begets further usage. This is a **symmetry-breaking** event: the uniform distribution is unstable under perturbation once a threshold density is crossed.

---

### Stage 2 — Phenomenological Anchoring (Naming and Ostension)

The concept acquires a name and a rough ostensive definition: "gene" as "the thing responsible for a heritable trait." The field Ω begins to localize. Crucially, this stage is driven by the **nonlocal coupling terms** (J₁∗Ω) and (J₂∗D_σ′Ω) — the concept doesn't sharpen in isolation. It crystallizes through interaction with *other* concepts already present in the community's discourse (cell, trait, chromosome, species). Convolution here captures how meaning at one point in conceptual space is shaped by meaning at neighboring and distant points.

The **gauge field** B(σ,τ) in the covariant derivative D_σ = ∂_σ − i(ρ/κ)B plays a key role: it represents the *institutional and disciplinary context* that bends conceptual trajectories. The same pre-conceptual intuition, embedded in different disciplinary "gauge fields," would crystallize differently — compare Mendel's "factor," Johannsen's "gene," and the modern molecular gene.

---

### Stage 3 — Relational Structuring (Theoretical Integration)

The concept becomes embedded in a network of lawful relations: genes are on chromosomes, they segregate, they assort independently. This is the regime where the **harmonic forcing term** f_harm(σ,τ) dominates — the Fourier decomposition Σₙ[(cₙ/n)Ω̂ₙ + (dₙ/n²)(ipₙ)Ω̂ₙ]e^{ipₙσ} represents the concept being decomposed into and reconstructed from its *relational harmonics*. Each mode n corresponds to a different structural relationship the concept participates in. The 1/n and 1/n² weighting encodes a key epistemological fact: **low-order relations (broad structural features) stabilize first; fine-grained distinctions crystallize later.**

The transition into this stage is driven by the **global coupling** g_glob H(τ)Ω, where H(τ) = ∫|Ω|²dσ. As the total "conceptual mass" of the field grows (the community invests more work in the concept), the global feedback strengthens, pulling the concept into tighter coherence with the total theoretical structure. This is *holistic* constraint: the concept must be consistent with everything else that's known.

---

### Stage 4 — Formalization (Mathematical/Operational Precision)

The concept achieves a precise formal definition: a gene is a sequence of nucleotides encoding a functional product, or (in population genetics) an allele frequency in a formal model. The field Ω has now collapsed to a sharp, stable, coherent state — a near-eigenstate of the system's effective Hamiltonian.

The **variational structure** of the first equation (the action-like expression ⟨Ω, ∂Ω⟩ + ...) becomes relevant here: a fully formalized concept is one that **extremizes** a functional — it is a *stationary point* where the concept can no longer be improved by small perturbations without increasing "cost." The quadratic and interaction terms represent the competing pressures of:

- **Internal coherence** (the ∧ and ★ terms — self-consistency under different algebraic operations on the concept)
- **Empirical fit** (the B²(σ) term — coupling to the observable "background field")
- **Structural economy** (the harmonic series — preferring explanations with fewer independent parameters)

---

### Stage 5 — Dissipation and Renewal

The decay term −iδ(ρ,β_h)Ω is not pathological — it's essential. It represents the fact that **no concept is permanently stable.** As new phenomena fall outside the concept's explanatory bandwidth β_h, or as the "harmonic resistance" ρ grows (the concept becomes rigid against needed revision), energy leaks from the localized state. The gene concept has already undergone this: the "one gene → one protein" picture decayed under pressure from splicing, regulatory RNA, epigenetics, and distributed genetic effects. The concept doesn't vanish — it *delocalizes partially* and re-enters Stage 2 or 3 for a new pass through the cycle.

The deep insight the formalism encodes: concept formation is not a *logical* process (deduction from data) nor a purely *social* one (convention). It is a **dynamical phase transition** in a nonlinear, nonlocal field — governed by competing pressures of diffusion (ambiguity), self-interaction (usage reinforcement), coupling to context (disciplinary gauge fields), global coherence (theoretical integration), and dissipation (anomaly-driven revision). The concept "gene" didn't get *discovered* or *invented* — it **condensed**.
