

**Level 1 — Physical Chemistry**
Cellulose and lignin in wood undergo exothermic pyrolysis and oxidation when their temperature exceeds ~300 °C. The heat released by combustion radiates, convects, and conducts into adjacent fuel, raising it past the same ignition threshold. The fire propagates as a self-sustaining reaction front through a continuous medium of thermally accessible fuel.

**Level 2 — Thermodynamic / Systems**
A forest fire is a spatial chain reaction: an energy-releasing process at one site produces a flux that pushes neighboring sites past the activation barrier for the same process. Propagation requires (a) sufficient energy output per site, (b) proximity of the next fuel site, and (c) the next site's threshold being reachable by that flux. It halts when any of these three conditions fails.

**Level 3 — Network / Contagion**
A burning node activates its neighbors if the coupling strength exceeds each neighbor's threshold. The fire is percolation on a graph: spread depends on the density and connectivity of susceptible nodes and the transmission probability along each edge. It maps exactly onto SIR-type epidemic dynamics on a lattice.

**Level 4 — Formal Dynamical**
A local transition (off → on) emits a field that drives the same transition in spatially coupled elements. The system is a reaction–diffusion process: a nonlinear local source term coupled to a spatial transport operator. Propagation is the existence of a traveling-wave solution with positive velocity, determined by the ratio of the reaction rate to the diffusion/transport rate.

**Level 5 — Abstract Principle**
**A local transformation that produces the conditions for the same transformation in its neighborhood will propagate through any medium where neighborhoods overlap.** This is the minimal, substrate-independent statement: *self-enabling local change + coupling = global spread.* It is indifferent to whether the substrate is wood, neurons, opinions, or lattice sites — only the structure (local rule + connectivity + threshold) matters.

---

The invariant across all five levels: **the output of the process at one locus is the input that triggers the identical process at an adjacent locus, and continuity of the medium closes the loop.**
