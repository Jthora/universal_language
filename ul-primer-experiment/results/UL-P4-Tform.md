

## From Vague Intuition to Formal Object: The Meaning-Formation of 'Gene'

Reading the framework you've presented as a **semiotic field theory** — where $S(\xi, \tau)$ is a meaning-signal evolving over an interpretive coordinate $\xi$ and time $\tau$ — the stages and driving forces of concept-formation map onto distinct dynamical regimes of the equation:

$$i\eta_s D_\xi S = \frac{\eta_s^2}{2\chi} D_\xi^2 S + g_{\text{loc}}|S|^2 S + g_{\text{glob}}E(\xi)S + (L_1 * S) + D_\xi^\dagger(L_2 * D_{\xi'}S) + f_{\text{form}} - i\mu(\eta_s, \xi_i)S$$

---

### Stage 1 — Diffuse Pre-Conceptual Field

Before 'gene' exists as a word, the semiotic field $S(\xi,\tau)$ is **low-amplitude and broadly distributed** across interpretive space. Breeders and naturalists sense that *something* is inherited, but the signal has no localization. Semantic inertia $\eta_s$ is high — existing frameworks (blending inheritance, vital forces) resist reorganization. The dominant term is the kinetic/dispersive one $\frac{\eta_s^2}{2\chi}D_\xi^2 S$: meaning spreads out rather than concentrating. The ratio $\eta_s / \xi_i$ is large, so the compact expression $(A^f \times (\Sigma_4 + P)) - (\eta_s/\xi_i)$ remains negative — no transition is yet possible.

**What's happening:** Scattered observations (resemblance of offspring to parents, horticultural lore) exist as unconnected meaning-fragments with no coherent phase structure.

---

### Stage 2 — Metaphorical Condensation (Convolution Kernels Activate)

The **non-local convolution terms** $(L_1 * S)$ and $D_\xi^\dagger(L_2 * D_{\xi'}S)$ begin to matter. These represent *analogical and metaphorical transport* — meaning carried from neighboring conceptual domains into the forming concept. Mendel's "Elemente" or "Faktoren" borrow structure from atomistic thinking in chemistry and physics. The convolution kernels $L_1, L_2$ act as bridges: they allow meaning at distant points $\xi'$ to influence the local signal.

The field begins to **cluster**. It is no longer uniform noise — there are now regions of higher $|S|^2$, nascent peaks in interpretive space. But the concept is still metaphorical, carried by analogy rather than anchored to its own empirical ground.

**Driver of transition:** Cross-domain analogical reasoning lowers the effective interpretive friction $\xi_i$, beginning to shift the $\eta_s/\xi_i$ ratio.

---

### Stage 3 — Empirical Coupling and Phase Transition

The covariant derivative $D_\xi = \partial_\xi - i(\chi/\eta_s)V(\xi,\tau)$ is the key structural feature here. The **external potential** $V(\xi,\tau)$ represents the empirical landscape — experimental results, observations, breeding data. As Mendel's ratios are rediscovered (de Vries, Correns, Tschermak ~1900), and as Johannsen coins "gene" (1909), the coupling $\chi/\eta_s$ *increases*: empirical reality exerts stronger gauge-like force on the meaning-field.

This is the **phase transition** $P$ in $(\Sigma_4 + P)$. The four-phase semiotic structure $\Sigma_4$ can be read as the concept passing through four meaning-phases:

| Phase | Character | 'Gene' instance |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Ø → Intuitive** | Felt but unarticulated | Breeders' tacit knowledge |
| **Intuitive → Nominal** | Named, metaphorical | Mendel's "Faktoren," Johannsen's "gene" |
| **Nominal → Operational** | Experimentally tractable | Morgan's chromosomal mapping |
| **Operational → Formal** | Mathematically/structurally precise | Molecular gene (Watson-Crick), sequence-defined ORF |

The transition between phases 2→3 is driven by the potential $V$ overcoming inertia: experiments *compel* the meaning-field to localize around specific interpretive regions. The damping term $-i\mu(\eta_s, \xi_i)S$ dissipates unstable meaning-modes — competing definitions die off.

---

### Stage 4 — Nonlinear Self-Reinforcement

Once the field has localized enough, the **nonlinear self-interaction** $g_{\text{loc}}|S|^2 S$ becomes dominant. This is the critical feedback loop: *the more precise the concept, the more it reinforces its own precision.* A well-defined 'gene' lets you design experiments that further refine 'gene.' The local meaning-density $|S|^2$ acts as its own potential well.

Simultaneously, the **global coupling** $g_{\text{glob}}E(\xi)S$ where $E(\xi)=\int_0^Z |S|^2 d\xi$ means the concept's meaning is shaped by the *total accumulated meaning-mass* of the entire semiotic field — the gene concept doesn't exist in isolation but is stabilized by its relationships to the entire web of biological concepts (chromosome, allele, phenotype, DNA).

**What's happening:** Morgan's group maps genes to chromosomal loci. The concept becomes self-reinforcing — each successful prediction deepens the interpretive well.

### Stage 5 — Formal Crystallization (Fourier Forcing)

The forcing term

$$f_{\text{form}}(\xi,\tau) = \sum_{n\geq 1}\left[\frac{a_n(\tau)}{n}\hat{S}_n + \frac{b_n(\tau)}{n^2}(iz_n)\hat{S}_n\right]e^{iz_n\xi}$$

drives the signal toward **discrete harmonic modes**. This represents *formalization* — the concept acquiring explicit mathematical and structural expression. The $1/n$ and $1/n^2$ decay ensures that **low-order modes dominate**: the core formal features of the concept (a gene is a sequence encoding a functional product; it has a locus, allelic variants, regulatory regions) are the strongest attractors, while higher-order refinements (epigenetic modifications, alternative splicing, gene regulatory networks) enter as progressively weaker corrections.

The Fourier coefficients $\hat{S}_n = \frac{1}{Z}\int_0^Z e^{-iz_n\xi'}S(\xi')d\xi'$ are the concept *projected onto formal basis functions* — this is literally the extraction of discrete, communicable structural features from the continuous meaning-field.

**What's happening:** The molecular revolution (1953→). 'Gene' acquires a physical referent (DNA segment), a formal notation (sequence), a calculus (molecular biology's central dogma), and eventually a computational representation (GenBank entries, ORF predictions).

---

### Stage 6 — Solitonic Stability: $\Psi_\infty$

The endpoint $\Psi_\infty$ is a **stable, self-localizing soliton** — a meaning-state that maintains coherence against perturbation. The balance between dispersion ($D_\xi^2 S$), nonlinear self-trapping ($|S|^2 S$), empirical anchoring ($V$), and formal forcing ($f_{\text{form}}$) produces a solution that propagates through the scientific community without losing its shape.

But $\Psi_\infty$ is an *asymptotic* limit, not a fixed point. The gene concept today is under fresh dispersive pressure (ENCODE, non-coding RNA, epigenetics), which can be read as new terms in $V(\xi,\tau)$ and perturbations to $f_{\text{form}}$ threatening to de-localize the soliton — potentially initiating a new cycle.

---

### What Drives the Transitions?

Reading across the full dynamics, three forces drive stage-transitions:

1. **Decreasing $\eta_s / \xi_i$** — Semantic inertia is overcome by active community engagement (new experiments, new practitioners, institutional support). When this ratio drops below a critical threshold, the variational operator $\delta$ in the compact formula permits transition.

2. **Increasing empirical coupling $\chi$** — More and stronger experimental constraints tighten the gauge connection $D_\xi$, forcing the meaning-field to conform to the structure of the world. The concept stops being free to mean anything.

3. **Nonlinear threshold crossing** — Once $|S|^2$ exceeds a critical density, self-reinforcement ($g_{\text{loc}}|S|^2 S$) dominates over dispersion. This is the point of no return — the concept has enough internal coherence to sustain itself. Before this threshold, concepts can evaporate back into vagueness; after it, formalization becomes quasi-inevitable.

The deep insight of this framework is that meaning-formation is not a linear refinement but a **phase transition in a nonlinear field** — driven by the interplay between internal semantic dynamics, external empirical coupling, analogical transport, and formal attractors. The 'gene' didn't gradually sharpen; it *condensed*.
