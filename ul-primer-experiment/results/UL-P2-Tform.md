The formation of a scientific concept like 'gene' can be understood as a process with distinct stages, each driven by specific pressures that force the transition to the next.

---

## Stage 1: Pre-formal Seed — F⁰(∅)

The process begins with an empty carrier: some puzzling regularity that existing concepts fail to capture. Mendel observes discrete inheritance patterns; something is being transmitted, but there is no name for it, no boundary around it. This is $F^0(\emptyset)$ — the application of an organizing impulse ($F$) to a void. What exists is not yet a concept but a *felt gap*: a region of meaning-space $\alpha$ where $\Phi(\alpha)$ is near zero but where empirical pressure demands amplitude.

The intuition at this stage is carried by metaphor and analogy — "factors," "particles of heredity." These are borrowed structures from adjacent concepts, acting like the **convolution kernels** $W_1 * \Phi$ in the equation: nearby established meanings bleed into the empty region, giving the proto-concept its first rough shape.

---

## Stage 2: Iterative Accretion — F¹(∅) → F²(∅) → ⋯

Each encounter with data or argument applies $F$ again. Bateson coins "genetics"; Johannsen coins "gene" (1909) — but the word still refers to something whose material nature is unknown. Each iteration $F^n(\emptyset)$ adds:

- **Local self-interaction** ($g_{loc}|\Phi|^2\Phi$): the more a concept is used, the more it stabilizes its own usage. Scientists who adopt 'gene' generate further experimental designs that require it, reinforcing its amplitude locally in meaning-space.
- **Global normalization** ($g_{glob} R(\alpha)\Phi$, where $R(\alpha) = \int_0^\Lambda |\Phi|^2 d\alpha$): the total "weight" of the concept across all users and contexts feeds back. As the community grows, a consensus region emerges, even while local disagreements persist.

What *drives* this stage is recursion: each use of the concept generates results that demand its re-application. The Fourier-mode structure $f_{rec}(\alpha, \tau) = \sum_{k\geq 1} \frac{a_k(\tau)}{k} \hat{\Phi}_k e^{iq_k\alpha}$ captures this — the concept develops periodic internal structure. Different senses of 'gene' (Mendelian factor, unit of selection, cistron, etc.) emerge as distinct $k$-modes, each with its own amplitude $a_k$ and wavelength $\Lambda/k$.

---

## Stage 3: Differentiation and Conflict — The Gauge Field G(α,τ)

As the concept accretes meaning, internal tensions appear. The covariant derivative $D_\alpha = \partial_\alpha - i(\nu/\mu)G(\alpha,\tau)$ tells us that movement through meaning-space is no longer free — there is a *gauge field* $G$ that curves the conceptual landscape. You cannot naively translate 'gene' from molecular biology to population genetics without distortion.

This is the stage where rival formalizations compete. Is the gene a stretch of DNA? A functional unit? A unit of selection (as Dawkins would later argue)? Each formalization is a different local frame in the $\alpha$-space, and $G(\alpha,\tau)$ encodes the incompatibilities between them.

The **dissipative term** $-i\kappa(\mu_s, d_r)\Phi$ becomes critical: $\mu_s$ (structural stability) and $d_r$ (recursion depth) jointly determine whether the concept can sustain its own contradictions. If recursion depth is shallow relative to stability demands ($\mu_s / d_r$ is large), the concept loses coherence — it fragments or is abandoned. If the ratio is manageable, the concept survives into the next stage.

---

## Stage 4: Phase Transition — The Fixed Point Φ ≅ F(Φ)

At some critical moment, the iterative sequence converges. For 'gene,' this arguably occurs in two waves: first with the Watson-Crick structure (1953), which anchors the gene to a material substrate, and again with the development of molecular genetics' central dogma.

The convergence $\Phi \cong F(\Phi)$ means the concept has become *self-consistent*: applying the organizing function $F$ no longer changes it. It is now a fixed point. This is more than mere stability — it is **self-reference**: the concept can explain its own conditions of application. You can use molecular genetics to explain why genes produce the inheritance patterns that led us to posit genes.

What drives this transition is the interplay between:
- The **gradient terms** $(W_2 * D_{\alpha'}\Phi)$ — formal rigor demands that the concept vary smoothly across contexts, forcing coherence.
- The **nonlinear self-coupling** $|\Phi|^2\Phi$ — the concept's existing amplitude in the community makes it energetically costly to displace.
- The **recursive forcing** $f_{rec}$ — which keeps injecting structured perturbations at every scale $k$, preventing premature freezing at a crude approximation.

---

## Stage 5: Formal Crystallization — The Five-fold Morphology

Once the fixed point is reached, the concept acquires the character described by $(G^\Phi \times (D_5 + M)) - (\mu_s / d_r) \xrightarrow{\eta} \Xi_\infty$. The five-fold developmental morphology $D_5$ suggests the mature concept achieves multiple simultaneous formal realizations: the gene as information carrier, as physical molecule, as unit of selection, as regulatory element, as evolutionary marker. These are not competing definitions but *facets* of a single stable object, held together by morphic logic $M$ — the structural rules that govern how the facets interrelate.

The natural transformation $\eta$ maps this entire structure into $\Xi_\infty$, the space of fully formalized objects. The gene is no longer someone's vague intuition; it is an object with a place in multiple formal theories simultaneously.

---

## What Drives the Transitions

At each boundary between stages, the same core tension appears in different guise: **the concept's current form is insufficient to absorb the demands placed on it.** Formally:

1. **∅ → F⁰(∅)**: Empirical anomaly creates a vacuum in meaning-space.
2. **F^n → F^{n+1}**: Local self-reinforcement ($|\Phi|^2\Phi$) and community normalization ($R(\alpha)\Phi$) drive accretion, but new data introduces higher $k$-modes that the current shape cannot accommodate.
3. **Accretion → Conflict**: The gauge field $G$ grows until naive translation between contexts breaks down; the concept must either differentiate or fragment.
4. **Conflict → Fixed point**: The dissipation $\kappa(\mu_s, d_r)$ prunes unstable variants; the gradient and self-coupling terms force convergence; recursive forcing prevents collapse to triviality.
5. **Fixed point → Formal object**: The natural transformation $\eta$ completes the passage from self-consistent usage to explicit placement within a formal framework.

The entire trajectory is a **colimit** — each stage is a legitimate approximation, and the final concept is not any one of them but the universal object they all point toward. The concept 'gene' as we now deploy it is precisely this colimit: the unique (up to isomorphism) structure that all the intermediate stages were partial views of.
