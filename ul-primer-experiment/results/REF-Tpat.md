

Here are three non-obvious structural parallels with formal correspondences:

---

**1. Mantle convection cells ↔ Capital circulation circuits**

In the mantle, thermal gradient between core and surface drives closed-loop Rayleigh-Bénard convection: hot material rises, displaces laterally, cools, densifies, sinks, reheats. In a national economy, return-on-investment gradients drive capital through an identical topological loop: capital flows toward high-return sectors, extracts yield (cooling), accumulates overhead (densification), exits, and re-enters the financial substrate for reallocation.

**Formal correspondence:** Both are dissipative systems governed by a continuity equation ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} = -\partial_t \rho$) where flux $\mathbf{J}$ is driven by a potential gradient ($\nabla T$ / $\nabla r$, where $r$ = rate of return). Both undergo spontaneous symmetry-breaking above a critical threshold (Rayleigh number $Ra > Ra_c$ / minimum surplus rate above subsistence). Below threshold: static stratification. Above: self-organizing cellular flow. The number and geometry of cells is set by the aspect ratio of the domain, not by the driving force — just as the sectoral structure of an economy is constrained by geographic and institutional boundary conditions, not merely by the magnitude of available capital.

---

**2. Subduction recycling ↔ Schumpeterian creative destruction**

Oceanic lithosphere accrues density as it ages (cooling → thermal contraction). Past a critical age (~80–200 Ma), slab-pull exceeds resistive forces and the plate subducts, recycling its material into the mantle where it eventually feeds new ridge volcanism. Industries accumulate overhead as they mature (bureaucratization, legacy infrastructure, regulatory capture). Past a critical overhead ratio, competitive pressure forces obsolescence; the constituent resources (labor, capital, patents) are recycled into the economic substrate and re-emerge in new sectors.

**Formal correspondence:** Both follow the same **age-dependent instability** dynamic, formalizable as: a material property $\rho(t)$ increases monotonically with age $t$, and the system undergoes a phase transition (subduction / firm death) when $\rho(t)$ crosses a threshold $\rho_c$ determined by the resisting medium. Crucially, in both systems this recycling is *not* loss — it is the mechanism that sustains the source (mid-ocean ridge / venture ecosystem). Remove subduction and ridge spreading halts; remove firm death and new firm formation collapses. Both obey a birth-death process where the destruction rate at the sink is coupled to the creation rate at the source via a conservation law on total material/capital stock.

---

**3. Transform fault accommodation ↔ Informal economy & regulatory arbitrage**

Plates cannot move in pure divergence/convergence because they are constrained to the surface of a sphere; transform faults arise as kinematic necessity to accommodate differential motion between plates on a curved surface (Euler's theorem — all plate motion is rotation about a pole). Analogously, formal economic sectors cannot fully absorb differential growth rates between industries constrained within a single national regulatory and monetary framework; informal economies, gray markets, and regulatory arbitrage arise as structural necessities to accommodate the differential.

**Formal correspondence:** Both are consequences of a **geometric constraint on motion within a closed manifold**. On a sphere, three plates meeting at a triple junction must satisfy a velocity closure condition ($\sum \mathbf{v}_i = 0$); transform faults are the degrees of freedom that allow this. In a bounded national economy, sectoral growth rates must satisfy aggregate accounting identities ($\sum \Delta Y_i = \Delta GDP$); informal sectors are the degrees of freedom that absorb residuals when formal sector rigidities prevent smooth accommodation. In both cases, the "fault" structure is not a failure — it is a mathematically necessary accommodation term. Its magnitude is predictable from the mismatch between the degrees of freedom of the driving forces and the constraints of the embedding manifold (spherical geometry / national institutional framework).
