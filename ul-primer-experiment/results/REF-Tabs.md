

## Five Levels of Abstraction

**Level 1 — Physical Chemistry**
Cellulose and lignin in dry wood undergo exothermic oxidation when their temperature exceeds ~300°C (flash point). This reaction releases radiant and convective heat sufficient to raise adjacent dry wood above the same threshold, initiating a self-propagating chain of combustion across contiguous fuel.

**Level 2 — Thermodynamic System**
A forest fire is a spatial autocatalytic process: an exothermic reaction at one site transfers enough thermal energy to its neighborhood to exceed the activation energy of the same reaction there. The fire spreads as a reaction–diffusion front, sustained as long as the local free energy released exceeds the energy required to ignite the next fuel element.

**Level 3 — Dynamical Propagation**
A local disturbance in a susceptible medium pushes neighboring elements past a critical threshold, each of which then pushes its own neighbors past the same threshold. The system exhibits a traveling wavefront whose velocity depends on the coupling strength (heat transfer) and the gap between the medium's resting state and its ignition threshold (how dry the wood is).

**Level 4 — Network Cascade**
An activated node in a connected network triggers its neighbors when the transmitted signal exceeds their activation threshold. Each newly activated node becomes a source, producing a cascade whose extent depends on connectivity (fuel continuity), transmission strength (heat output), and node susceptibility (dryness). This is structurally identical to epidemic spreading, neural avalanches, or information cascades.

**Level 5 — Abstract Principle**
**A local transition, once triggered, propagates through a coupled medium whenever the output of the transition at one site is sufficient to trigger the same transition at a neighboring site.** This is the general form: propagation occurs when *f(activated) ≥ threshold(neighbor)* across a connected domain. The specific substrate — wood, neurons, people, or ψ-fields — is irrelevant. The core truth is: *self-propagation requires only a local transformation that produces enough of its own trigger.*
