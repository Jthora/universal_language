

# Recursive Decomposition of *Democracy*

## Level 1 — Institutional Apparatus (Concrete)

**Components:** Elections · Legislatures · Courts · Constitutions · Parties · Free Press · Bureaucracies

**Composition rule:** These institutions interlock through *procedural legitimacy* — elections authorize legislatures, constitutions constrain all organs, courts adjudicate boundaries, press monitors compliance, parties aggregate preferences into actionable blocs. The ensemble produces **a functioning democratic state** when each institution's output is a required input for at least one other.

---

## Level 2 — Procedural Mechanisms

**Components:** Majority rule · Minority protection · Periodic succession · Deliberation · Representation · Transparency · Accountability

**Composition rule:** Each Level 1 institution is an *instantiation* of one or more of these procedures. Elections instantiate majority rule + periodic succession. Courts instantiate minority protection. Legislatures instantiate deliberation + representation. The press instantiates transparency. Impeachment and judicial review instantiate accountability. The procedures compose upward by providing the **operating logic** that makes institutions more than empty shells — without periodic succession, an "election" is a plebiscite; without minority protection, a "court" is a tribunal.

---

## Level 3 — Normative Principles

**Components:** Political equality · Popular sovereignty · Liberty · Pluralism · Rule of law

**Composition rule:** Each Level 2 procedure is *justified by* and *derived from* a subset of these principles. Majority rule follows from popular sovereignty + political equality. Minority protection follows from liberty + pluralism. Rule of law underwrites all procedures by demanding that power operates through stable, general norms rather than arbitrary decree. The principles compose upward by supplying the **normative warrant** — they answer *why* a procedure is democratically legitimate rather than merely conventional.

---

## Level 4 — Philosophical Commitments

**Components:** Moral equality of persons · Fallibilism (no agent has privileged access to truth) · Rational agency · Communicative reason

**Composition rule:** The Level 3 principles are entailments of these deeper commitments. Political equality is the *political surface* of moral equality of persons. Popular sovereignty arises because fallibilism denies any elite a permanent claim to rule — if no one is epistemically privileged, authority must be distributed. Liberty presupposes rational agency (a being that can choose). Pluralism is the social consequence of fallibilism + rational agency: rational agents, reasoning under uncertainty, will inevitably diverge. Deliberation rests on communicative reason — the assumption that reasons can be exchanged and assessed across perspectives. These commitments compose upward as **grounding conditions**: remove moral equality and political equality collapses; remove fallibilism and popular sovereignty becomes optional.

---

## Level 5 — Abstract Primitives (Philosophical Bedrock)

**Components:** *Autonomy* (self-legislation) · *Recognition* (mutual acknowledgment of subjectivity) · *Contingency* (non-necessity of any social order)

**Composition rule:**

- **Autonomy** generates rational agency and, through it, the entire liberty branch.
- **Recognition** generates moral equality — to recognize another as a subject is to concede their equal standing as a source of claims.
- **Contingency** generates fallibilism — if no social arrangement is metaphysically necessary, then all arrangements are revisable, and no authority is final.

These three primitives compose upward through a single operation: *autonomy constrained by mutual recognition under conditions of contingency* **= the democratic impulse itself**. Each higher level is a progressive concretization of this kernel — from commitments, to principles, to procedures, to institutions — gaining determinacy at each step while narrowing the space of possibilities.

---

### Composition Summary (bottom → top)

```
L5  Autonomy × Recognition × Contingency
      ↓  concretize as
L4  Moral Equality · Fallibilism · Rational Agency · Communicative Reason
      ↓  entail
L3  Political Equality · Popular Sovereignty · Liberty · Pluralism · Rule of Law
      ↓  justify
L2  Majority Rule · Minority Protection · Succession · Deliberation · Representation
      ↓  instantiate as
L1  Elections · Legislatures · Courts · Constitutions · Parties · Press
```

Each upward arrow is a *many-to-one* mapping: multiple lower components compose (conjunctively) to produce each higher component, and each higher component can be *decomposed* back into its constituent primitives — making the structure fully recursive and auditable at every level.
