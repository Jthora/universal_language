

# Recursive Decomposition of *Democracy*

## Level 1 — Institutional Machinery (Concrete)

**Components:** Elections, Legislatures, Courts, Constitutions, Parties, Free Press, Bureaucracies

**Composition rule:** These interlock as a system of *procedural constraints*: elections populate legislatures, legislatures produce statutes bounded by constitutions, courts adjudicate conflicts between statutes and constitutions, parties aggregate preferences into contestable platforms, free press provides the informational substrate for evaluation, and bureaucracies execute policy. Together they compose into → **self-correcting governance under law**.

---

## Level 2 — Structural Principles

**Components:** Separation of powers, Rule of law, Periodic accountability, Majority decision with minority protection, Federalism/subsidiarity

**Composition rule:** Each principle is a *design constraint* on institutional form. Separation of powers prevents monopoly of coercion. Rule of law makes power predictable and contestable. Periodic accountability creates a feedback loop (the governed can replace governors). Majority rule resolves collective choices; minority protection prevents the feedback loop from collapsing into tyranny. Subsidiarity distributes decision-rights to the scale at which information is richest. These compose upward into Level 1: every institution above is an instantiation of one or more of these principles. **Collectively they produce → distributed, self-limiting authority.**

---

## Level 3 — Normative Commitments

**Components:** Political equality, Popular sovereignty, Liberty (negative and positive), Pluralism, Deliberative rationality

**Composition rule:** Political equality (one person, one unit of standing) is the *symmetry axiom* — it demands that authority structures treat participants as interchangeable in standing. Popular sovereignty locates the *source* of legitimate force in the collective. Liberty defines a *boundary* the collective may not cross. Pluralism acknowledges irreducible diversity of ends. Deliberative rationality requires that collective choices be justifiable by reasons, not merely aggregated from preferences. These norms compose upward into Level 2: each structural principle is a *mechanism that satisfies* one or more normative commitments (e.g., periodic accountability operationalises popular sovereignty; minority protection operationalises liberty + pluralism). **Collectively they produce → the moral grammar of legitimate collective decision.**

---

## Level 4 — Epistemic and Relational Foundations

**Components:** Mutual recognition (each agent recognises every other as an end, not merely a means), Fallibilism (no agent or group has guaranteed access to truth), Reciprocity (willingness to be bound by terms one offers to others), Communicative capacity (agents can exchange reasons)

**Composition rule:** Mutual recognition generates the demand for political equality. Fallibilism generates the demand for pluralism and deliberation — because no one is provably right, decisions must remain revisable and contestable. Reciprocity generates the constraint that power must be self-limiting (you accept constraints you impose). Communicative capacity is the *medium* through which recognition, fallibility, and reciprocity become operational rather than merely notional. These compose upward into Level 3: each normative commitment is a *projection* of these relational facts into the political domain. **Collectively they produce → the intersubjective field in which normative claims can be raised, contested, and redeemed.**

---

## Level 5 — Philosophical Primitives (Abstract)

**Components:** **Agency** (the capacity to act for reasons), **Plurality** (the irreducible fact of distinct perspectives), **Finitude** (bounded knowledge, bounded life)

**Composition rule:** Agency is the atom — without it, there is no one to govern or be governed. Plurality is the condition that makes politics *necessary* (a single agent needs no governance structure). Finitude is the condition that makes democracy *specifically* necessary rather than some other regime: because knowledge is bounded, no philosopher-king solution is stable; because life is bounded, each agent's stake is urgent and non-deferrable. Agency × Plurality generates the *problem* (how to coordinate distinct wills). Agency × Finitude generates the *constraint* (the coordination mechanism must be self-correcting, since no design is provably final). Plurality × Finitude generates the *ethic* (each finite perspective deserves standing precisely because no perspective is total). **Together these three primitives compose upward into Level 4: mutual recognition is what Agency grants to Plurality; fallibilism is what Agency concedes to Finitude; reciprocity is the symmetry that Plurality imposes on Finitude; communicative capacity is Agency exercised across Plurality under Finitude.**

---

## The Full Composition Stack

```
L5  Agency × Plurality × Finitude
      ↓ compose into
L4  Mutual Recognition, Fallibilism, Reciprocity, Communicative Capacity
      ↓ project into political domain as
L3  Political Equality, Popular Sovereignty, Liberty, Pluralism, Deliberation
      ↓ operationalised by
L2  Separation of Powers, Rule of Law, Accountability, Majority+Minority, Subsidiarity
      ↓ instantiated as
L1  Elections, Legislatures, Courts, Constitutions, Parties, Press, Bureaucracies
      = Democracy (concrete)
```

Each level is *underdetermined* by the level below it — the same primitives could, in principle, compose differently (and historically have: theocracy also acknowledges finitude but resolves it via revelation rather than fallibilism). What distinguishes the democratic composition path is that it *preserves symmetry all the way up*: no level introduces an asymmetry of standing that isn't already justified by the level beneath it.
