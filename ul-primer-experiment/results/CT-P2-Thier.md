

## Hierarchical Decomposition of Democracy

### Level 5 (Most Concrete): Institutional Mechanisms

**Components:** elections, legislatures, courts, constitutions, bureaucracies, political parties, free press, civil service

**Composition → Level 4:** These mechanisms compose through procedural linkage. Elections feed into legislatures; legislatures produce statutes interpreted by courts; constitutions constrain all three; parties aggregate preferences into electoral choices; press provides the information channel closing the feedback loop. The composite is a functioning system of *governance structures*.

---

### Level 4: Governance Structures

**Components:** representation, separation of powers, rule of law, accountability cycles, federated authority

**Composition → Level 3:** Representation maps citizen preferences into decision-making bodies. Separation of powers partitions authority so no single node dominates. Rule of law ensures the system's outputs bind uniformly. Accountability cycles (election → office → review → election) create temporal feedback. These compose into the operational principle of *collective self-governance* — the property that a polity's rules are authored by those subject to them.

---

### Level 3: Collective Self-Governance

**Components:** popular sovereignty, consent of the governed, deliberation, majority rule tempered by minority protection

**Composition → Level 2:** Popular sovereignty asserts the group as the origin of authority. Consent provides the legitimation morphism from individual to collective. Deliberation is the process by which disparate inputs are refined into coherent collective judgment. Majority rule with minority protection resolves the tension between decisiveness and inclusion. Together these compose into the deeper commitment: *political equality* — each person counts as one source of legitimate authority.

---

### Level 2: Political Equality

**Components:** moral equality of persons, individual autonomy (self-determination), reciprocity (mutual recognition)

**Composition → Level 1:** Moral equality asserts no person is inherently subordinate to another. Autonomy grants each person standing as an originator of claims and choices. Reciprocity requires that autonomy be symmetrically acknowledged — my freedom is conditioned on recognizing yours. These three compose into the foundational primitive: *equal rational agency* — the idea that each individual possesses the capacity and the right to participate in the determination of shared norms.

---

### Level 1 (Most Abstract): Equal Rational Agency

**Components (philosophical primitives):**
- **Agency** — the capacity to act on reasons
- **Rationality** — the capacity to evaluate, revise, and justify norms
- **Equality** — the symmetric attribution of these capacities across persons

This is the terminal level. No further political decomposition applies; these are metaphysical and ethical primitives. Agency and rationality compose (an agent that can reason about norms) and equality universalizes the result across all persons.

---

### The compositional chain, read bottom-up

$$
\text{Equal Rational Agency}
\xrightarrow{\text{symmetrize}}
\text{Political Equality}
\xrightarrow{\text{operationalize}}
\text{Collective Self-Governance}
\xrightarrow{\text{institutionalize}}
\text{Governance Structures}
\xrightarrow{\text{instantiate}}
\text{Institutional Mechanisms}
$$

Each arrow is a "structure-preserving map" in the informal sense: the constraints at Level $n$ must be faithfully carried into Level $n+1$. When an institution violates the equality primitive (Level 1), the composition is broken — the surface mechanism no longer correctly instantiates democracy, much as a functor that fails to preserve identities is no functor at all.
