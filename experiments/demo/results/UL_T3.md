# A Formal Framework for Metaphorical Meaning-Generation

## 0. Motivation

Metaphor is not decorative language — it is the primary mechanism by which novel meaning enters a conceptual system. When we say "time is money," we do not merely compare two things; we project the inferential structure of one domain onto another, and the *tension* between fit and misfit generates new understanding. The framework below formalizes this process.

---

## 1. Primitive Structures

**Definition 1.1 (Conceptual Domain).** A *conceptual domain* is a tuple D = (E, R, μ, τ) where:
- E is a set of entities (the "objects" of the domain)
- R ⊆ E × E × Λ is a set of labeled relations, Λ a label set
- μ: P(R) → M is a *meaning functor* assigning meaning-values from a meaning space M to substructures of relations
- τ: R → [0,1] is a *salience weighting* — how cognitively prominent each relation is within the domain

**Definition 1.2 (Meaning Space).** The meaning space M is a metric space (M, d_M) equipped with:
- A partial order ≤ (abstraction hierarchy: m₁ ≤ m₂ means m₂ is a more abstract version of m₁)
- A composition operation ⊕: M × M → M (meaning-combination)
- A *tension metric* δ: M × M → ℝ≥0 measuring semantic incompatibility

**Definition 1.3 (Schema).** A *schema* of a domain D is a subgraph S ⊆ (E, R) together with its induced meaning μ(R|_S). Schemas are the portable structural units that metaphor transports.

---

## 2. The Metaphorical Mapping

**Definition 2.1 (Metaphor).** A *metaphor* Φ: D_S ⇝ D_T from source domain D_S to target domain D_T is a partial structure-preserving map consisting of:

Φ = (φ_E, φ_R, φ_μ)

where:
- φ_E: E_S ⇀ E_T is a partial injection on entities
- φ_R: R_S ⇀ R_T is a partial map on relations satisfying the **structural consistency constraint**: if (a, b, λ) ∈ R_S and φ_R is defined on it, then φ_R(a,b,λ) = (φ_E(a), φ_E(b), λ') for some λ'
- φ_μ: M_S → M_T is the induced meaning-transport map

The squiggly arrow ⇝ (rather than →) emphasizes that this is *partial* and *tension-bearing* — not a homomorphism.

**Definition 2.2 (Metaphorical Partiality).** The *partiality* of Φ decomposes the source domain into three regions:

R_S = R^map ⊔ R^unused ⊔ R^blocked

- R^map: relations that map successfully to the target
- R^unused: relations not activated by the metaphor (latent surplus)
- R^blocked: relations that *cannot* map due to structural incompatibility

This three-part decomposition is essential. Much of metaphor's generative power lives in the boundaries between these regions.

---

## 3. The Tension Field

Here is where meaning *creation* (not mere transfer) occurs.

**Definition 3.1 (Local Tension).** For each mapped relation r ∈ R^map, the *local tension* is:

T(r) = δ(μ_S({r}), μ_T({φ_R(r)}))

This measures the semantic "strain" at each point of the mapping — how much the source meaning resists fitting the target structure.

**Definition 3.2 (Tension Field).** The *tension field* of metaphor Φ is the function:

T_Φ: R^map → ℝ≥0, r ↦ T(r)

**Theorem 3.3 (Generative Tension Principle).** *Metaphorical meaning is generated in proportion to integrated tension that remains below a coherence threshold.* Formally, define:

G(Φ) = ∫_{R^map} T_Φ(r) · 𝟙[T_Φ(r) < θ_coh] dτ_S(r)

where θ_coh is the *coherence threshold* and dτ_S integrates with respect to salience weighting. Then:
- G(Φ) = 0: literal statement (no tension → no new meaning)
- G(Φ) moderate: live metaphor (productive tension → novel meaning)
- T_Φ(r) ≥ θ_coh everywhere: absurdity (tension exceeds coherence → meaning collapse)

This captures the empirical observation that metaphors die when they become too familiar (tension → 0) and fail when they are too alien (tension → ∞).

---

## 4. Emergent Meaning via Blending

When the tension field has been established, new meaning crystallizes in the target domain.

**Definition 4.1 (Blend Space).** The *blend* of metaphor Φ is a new conceptual domain:

B_Φ = (E_B, R_B, μ_B, τ_B)

constructed as follows:
- E_B = im(φ_E) ∪ E_T^context (mapped entities plus target context)
- R_B includes three types of relations:
  1. **Preserved relations**: φ_R(R^map)
  2. **Emergent relations**: new r' ∉ R_S ∪ R_T generated by tension resolution
  3. **Inherited relations**: target-domain relations adjacent to mapped entities

**Definition 4.2 (Emergent Meaning).** The *emergent meaning* of metaphor Φ is:

E(Φ) = μ_B(R_B) ⊖ (μ_S(R_S) ⊕ μ_T(R_T))

where ⊖ denotes the meaning-space residual: the component of the blend's meaning that is not reducible to either source or target alone. This is the *genuinely new* meaning that exists nowhere except in the metaphor itself.

---

## 5. Dynamics: Metaphor as Process

Metaphorical meaning-making is not instantaneous. It unfolds.

**Definition 5.1 (Metaphor Evolution Equation).** Let ψ(x, t) represent the *meaning-state* over conceptual space x at cognitive time t. The evolution of meaning under active metaphorical processing is governed by:

iħ_cog ∂ψ/∂t = -(ħ²_cog/2m_cog) ∇²_x ψ + V_tension(x)ψ + g|ψ|²ψ

where:
- ħ_cog is the "cognitive action quantum" — the minimal unit of meaning-shift
- m_cog is cognitive inertia (resistance to conceptual restructuring)
- ∇²_x ψ is diffusion through conceptual space (spreading activation)
- V_tension(x) = T_Φ(x) is the tension field acting as a potential
- g|ψ|²ψ is the nonlinear self-interaction: meaning-states that are already concentrated attract further meaning (conceptual gravity)

This gives a **nonlinear Schrödinger-type equation for meaning dynamics** — connecting to the formalism in the provided context. The nonlinearity is essential: it captures how metaphorical meaning *self-organizes* rather than merely accumulating.

**Definition 5.2 (Spectral Decomposition of Meaning).** Expanding in Fourier modes over a bounded conceptual space [0, L]:

ψ(x,t) = Σ_{n=1}^∞ cₙ(t) e^{ikₙx}, kₙ = 2πn/L

Each mode n represents a *harmonic of meaning* — mode 1 is the dominant semantic content, higher modes encode finer structural nuances, connotations, and resonances. Metaphor activates higher modes that literal language leaves dormant.

## 6. The Category of Metaphors

**Definition 6.1 (Category Met).** Define the category of metaphors:
- **Objects**: conceptual domains D
- **Morphisms**: metaphors Φ: D_S ⇝ D_T
- **Composition**: for Φ: D_A ⇝ D_B and Ψ: D_B ⇝ D_C, the composite Ψ ∘ Φ: D_A ⇝ D_C is the *extended metaphor* (mapping through an intermediate domain)
- **Identity**: the trivial metaphor id_D (literal meaning, zero tension)

**Proposition 6.2.** *Composition of metaphors is not associative in general.* The blend space B_{Ψ ∘ Φ} depends on the *order* in which tensions are resolved, yielding:

E((Ψ ∘ Φ) ∘ Ξ) ≠ E(Ψ ∘ (Φ ∘ Ξ))

in the generic case. Thus **Met** is not a strict category but a *weak 2-category* (bicategory), where associativity holds only up to a natural isomorphism — a *coherence metaphor* mediating between two paths of metaphorical composition.

This is not a defect but a *feature*: it formalizes the empirical fact that the order in which metaphors are layered changes what they mean. "Life is a journey and a battle" differs from "Life is a battle and a journey."

---

## 7. Completeness and Universality

**Theorem 7.1 (Metaphorical Completeness).** *For any two conceptual domains D₁, D₂ with non-trivial schema overlap, there exists a metaphor Φ: D₁ ⇝ D₂ with positive emergent meaning: E(Φ) ≠ 0.*

*Proof sketch.* Any two domains with shared abstract schemas (which exist by virtue of shared grounding in embodied experience: containment, motion, force, balance) admit a partial structure-preserving map. Non-isomorphism of the domains guarantees non-zero tension. Moderate tension below the coherence threshold guarantees non-zero G(Φ). Non-zero generative tension produces emergent relations in the blend space by Definition 4.1. □

**Corollary 7.2.** *Metaphor is a universal meaning-generation mechanism.* Any meaning expressible in one domain can, in principle, seed novel meaning in any other sufficiently structured domain.

## 8. Classification of Metaphor Types

The framework induces a natural taxonomy based on tension-field geometry:

| Type | Tension Profile | G(Φ) | Example |
|------|----------------|-------|---------|
| **Dead metaphor** | T ≈ 0 everywhere | ≈ 0 | "Table *leg*" |
| **Conventional metaphor** | Low, uniform T | Low | "Spend *time*" |
| **Novel metaphor** | Peaked, localized T | Moderate | "Grief is an *anchor*" |
| **Extended metaphor** | Sustained, multi-modal T | High | Allegory, sustained analogy |
| **Catachresis** | T near θ_coh | Maximal | "The *sound* of blue" (synesthetic) |
| **Mixed metaphor** | Discontinuous T (competing fields) | Incoherent | "We'll *burn* that bridge when we *cross* it" |

---

## 9. Dissipation and Conventionalization

**Definition 9.1 (Metaphor Decay).** Over repeated use, tension dissipates:

∂T_Φ/∂t = -γ(σ, β) T_Φ + η(t)

where:
- γ(σ, β) > 0 is a damping coefficient depending on cultural saturation σ and communicative frequency β
- η(t) is a stochastic reactivation term (novel contexts, poetic defamiliarization)

As T → 0, metaphor becomes literal: the mapping loses its generative tension, and the blend collapses into the target domain. The emergent meaning either dies or is absorbed into the conventional meaning of the target — which is how metaphor *expands language itself*.

## 10. Summary: The Architecture of Metaphorical Meaning

```
SOURCE DOMAIN                    TARGET DOMAIN
    D_S ─────────Φ──────────→ D_T
     │          ╱    ╲           │
     │    R^map   R^blocked      │
     │      ╱        ╲           │
     │   Tension     Incoherence │
     │   Field T_Φ               │
     │      │                    │
     │      ▼                    │
     │  ┌────────────┐           │
     │  │ BLEND D_B  │           │
     │  │            │           │
     │  │ Emergent   │           │
     └──│ Meaning    │───────────┘
        │ E(Φ) ≠ 0  │
        └────────────┘
              │
              ▼
        Novel Understanding
```

**The core insight**: Metaphor creates meaning not despite the imperfection of the mapping, but *because of it*. Perfect maps are literal. Zero maps are nonsense. It is the structured partial failure of mapping — the tension field — that opens a space where meaning that existed in neither source nor target alone can crystallize. The formal framework captures this with:

1. **Partiality** (§2) — the map must be incomplete
2. **Tension** (§3) — incompleteness generates a measurable semantic force
3. **Nonlinear dynamics** (§5) — meaning self-organizes under tension
4. **Emergence** (§4) — the blend contains irreducible novelty
5. **Universality** (§7) — this mechanism works between any two structured domains
6. **Decay** (§9) — live metaphors die, expanding literal language as they go

Metaphor is, in this framework, the fundamental *engine of semantic expansion* — the process by which the space of expressible meanings grows.
