# Known Connections Reference Set for T4

**Task T4:** "What are the fundamental limits of what can be expressed in any language?"

## Known Connections (Not Novel)

### Formal/Mathematical Limits
1. **Gödel's incompleteness theorems** — no consistent formal system can express all true arithmetic statements; there are truths that cannot be proven
2. **Tarski's undefinability theorem** — truth for a language cannot be defined within that language
3. **Church-Turing thesis and the halting problem** — certain questions about computation are undecidable
4. **Chomsky hierarchy limits** — each level of the hierarchy has strictly limited expressive power; natural languages are (roughly) mildly context-sensitive
5. **Kolmogorov complexity / incompressibility** — some information cannot be compressed; random strings cannot be described more efficiently than by reproducing them
6. **Löwenheim-Skolem theorem** — first-order languages cannot distinguish different infinite cardinalities; inherent expressive limitations of first-order logic
7. **Lindström's theorem** — first-order logic is the strongest logic satisfying both compactness and downward Löwenheim-Skolem
8. **Descriptive set theory hierarchies** — arithmetic vs. analytic hierarchies rank definability/expressibility
9. **Beth definability and Craig interpolation** — what can be implicitly defined can be explicitly defined (within certain logics)

### Philosophical Limits
10. **Wittgenstein's Tractatus 7** — "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent" — the mystical lies beyond language
11. **Wittgenstein's later work** — language games, forms of life, the limits are practical/contextual not formally definable
12. **Sapir-Whorf hypothesis** — linguistic relativity: different languages may make different aspects of reality expressible/inexpressible
13. **Quine's indeterminacy of translation** — radical translation is indeterminate; meaning is not uniquely determined by behavior
14. **The problem of ineffability** — certain experiences (qualia, mystical states, aesthetic responses) seem to resist linguistic expression
15. **Davidson's conceptual schemes** — questioning whether "incommensurable" conceptual schemes are coherent
16. **Nagel's "What is it like to be a bat?"** — subjective character of experience may be beyond objective/linguistic description
17. **Derrida on supplementarity** — meaning always defers; language is an incomplete system that points beyond itself

### Cognitive / Empirical
18. **Color term limits (Berlin & Kay)** — languages differ in color vocabulary but follow universal patterns
19. **Embodiment constraints** — language is grounded in bodily experience, potentially limiting what disembodied entities can express
20. **Poverty of the stimulus / universal grammar** — innate constraints on what languages CAN be (which also constrains what CAN be expressed)
21. **Theory of mind limitations** — language for mental states limited by our ability to model others' minds

### Information-Theoretic
22. **Channel capacity** — finite bandwidth of any communication channel limits expression rate
23. **Rate-distortion theory** — expressing information within constraints always involves loss
24. **Semantic information** — Floridi: meaningful information = well-formed + truthful data; limits on what can be meaningful
