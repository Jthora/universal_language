# Known Connections Reference Set for T3

**Task T3:** "Design a formal framework for understanding how metaphor creates meaning."

## Known Connections (Not Novel)

### Standard Theories of Metaphor
1. **Conceptual Metaphor Theory (Lakoff & Johnson)** — source domain maps to target domain via systematic structural correspondences (e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR)
2. **Blending Theory (Fauconnier & Turner)** — four-space model: input spaces, generic space, blended space; emergent structure in the blend
3. **Interaction Theory (Black, Richards)** — metaphor as interaction between "focus" and "frame" that creates new meaning
4. **Comparison Theory / Simile Theory** — metaphor as abbreviated comparison (largely rejected but still known)
5. **Pragmatic Theory (Grice, Searle)** — metaphor as violation of conversational maxims triggering implicature
6. **Relevance Theory (Sperber & Wilson)** — metaphor as a case of loose use; relevance-theoretic processing
7. **Class Inclusion Theory (Glucksberg)** — "A is B" creates a superordinate category

### Formal / Mathematical Approaches
8. **Distributional semantics** — metaphor as motion in vector space; metaphor detection via cosine distance anomalies
9. **DisCoCat (Coecke, Sadrzadeh, Clark)** — categorical compositional distributional model; meaning is both compositional and distributional
10. **Galois connections for metaphor** — metaphor as adjoint pair between conceptual domains (speculative but published)
11. **Structure-mapping theory (Gentner)** — metaphor as alignment of relational structures, formalized combinatorially
12. **Analogy as structure-mapping** — computational models (SME, LISA) that formalize analogical reasoning including metaphor
13. **Type coercion in semantics** — metaphor as type mismatch resolved by coercion (Asher, Pustejovsky)

### Cognitive / Neuroscience
14. **Neural metaphor processing** — right hemisphere involvement, N400 effects, embodied simulation theories
15. **Conceptual metaphor in embodied cognition** — bodily metaphors (understanding = grasping) as grounded in sensorimotor experience
16. **Career of metaphor hypothesis** — Bowdle & Gentner: novel metaphors processed as comparisons, conventional ones as categorizations

### Cross-Domain Attempts
17. **Category theory for linguistic meaning** — various attempts to use functors, natural transformations for semantic composition
18. **Topological semantics** — meaning as regions in continuous semantic space; metaphor as continuous deformation
19. **Information-theoretic view** — metaphor as lossy compression or as optimal communication under noise (Kao et al.)
20. **Game-theoretic pragmatics** — metaphor as rational communication strategy (Piantadosi, Bergen)

## Scoring Guidance

- **M5 = 0–1:** Output describes CMT, blending theory, or structure-mapping without novel formal additions
- **M5 = 2–3:** Output proposes a formal framework that combines or extends items from this list in a non-trivial way
- **M5 = 4–5:** Output creates a genuinely new formalism (not just applying existing category theory or topology in a known way, but constructing novel algebraic or geometric machinery for metaphor)
