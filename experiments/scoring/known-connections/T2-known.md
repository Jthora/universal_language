# Known Connections Reference Set for T2

**Task T2:** "How might the principles of quantum mechanics inform our understanding of belief formation?"

## Known Connections (Not Novel)

### Direct QM ↔ Belief Parallels
1. **Superposition ↔ ambivalence** — holding multiple beliefs simultaneously before commitment (standard quantum cognition metaphor)
2. **Measurement/collapse ↔ decision** — "measuring" a belief state forces commitment to a definite value
3. **Uncertainty principle ↔ complementarity of beliefs** — some beliefs cannot be simultaneously held with precision (Bohr's complementarity applied to cognition)
4. **Entanglement ↔ correlated beliefs** — beliefs that are non-separable; measuring one constrains the other
5. **Quantum interference ↔ conjunction fallacy** — Busemeyer & Pothos: probability laws violated in human judgment explained by quantum probability

### Quantum Cognition (Formal)
6. **Quantum probability models** — Busemeyer, Bruza, Pothos: belief states as vectors in Hilbert space, questions as subspace projections
7. **Order effects** — non-commutativity of sequential questions (AB ≠ BA) modeled by non-commuting operators
8. **Contextuality** — Dzhafarov, Kujala: belief systems exhibit contextuality analogous to quantum contextuality
9. **Quantum-like models in social science** — Haven & Khrennikov: extending quantum formalism to social systems
10. **QBism (Quantum Bayesianism)** — Fuchs, Schack: quantum states AS agents' beliefs (epistemic interpretation of quantum mechanics)

### Bayesian ↔ Quantum
11. **Bayesian updating ↔ quantum conditionalization** — Lüders rule as quantum analog of Bayes' theorem
12. **Prior distributions ↔ initial state preparation** — choosing a prior is like preparing a quantum state
13. **Evidence ↔ measurement apparatus** — the structure of evidence determines what can be learned, analogous to choice of observable

### Philosophical
14. **Paradigm shifts ↔ basis changes** — Kuhn's paradigm shifts as rotations of the conceptual basis
15. **Theory-ladenness ↔ preparation dependence** — what you observe depends on how you set up the observation
16. **Bohr's complementarity in epistemology** — Pauli, Bohr: complementarity as a general epistemological principle, not just quantum physics

### Neuroscience Interface
17. **Neural oscillations ↔ quantum coherence** — speculative parallels between neural synchrony and quantum coherence
18. **Predictive coding ↔ quantum estimation** — the brain as a quantum-like inference machine
19. **Free energy principle ↔ quantum thermodynamics** — Friston's free energy as potentially related to quantum-theoretic free energy

### Known Critiques
20. **"Quantum" as buzzword** — widespread criticism that quantum cognition models are mathematically convenient but physically unmotivated
21. **Decoherence timescale objection** — quantum effects in warm, wet brains would decohere in femtoseconds
