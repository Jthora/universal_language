# Domain List for T4

**Task T4:** "What are the fundamental limits of what can be expressed in any language?"

## Pre-Specified Domains for M1 Scoring

| # | Domain | Examples of Substantive Engagement | Examples That Do NOT Count |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Mathematical logic / computability** | Gödel's incompleteness theorems, Church-Turing thesis, halting problem, decidability, Tarski's undefinability | "Some things can't be proven" |
| 2 | **Formal language theory** | Chomsky hierarchy (regular/context-free/context-sensitive/recursively enumerable), pumping lemma, automata theory | "Languages have different levels of complexity" |
| 3 | **Philosophy of language** | Wittgenstein's "limits of my language are the limits of my world," private language argument, Sapir-Whorf, ineffability, the mystical (Tractatus 7) | "Language has limits" |
| 4 | **Information theory** | Kolmogorov complexity, algorithmic randomness, incompressibility, rate-distortion theory, channel coding theorem | "Not everything can be communicated" |
| 5 | **Linguistics (typological/structural)** | Universal grammar, linguistic universals, parametric variation, color term universals (Berlin & Kay), morphological typology | "Languages differ in what they can express" |
| 6 | **Phenomenology / philosophy of mind** | Qualia, the explanatory gap, subjective experience, Nagel's bat, Chalmers' hard problem, the unsayable in experience | "Some experiences can't be described" |
| 7 | **Category theory / universal algebra** | Universal constructions, representable functors, adjoint functor theorem, structural expressiveness of different categorical frameworks | Only if used to model expressiveness limits |
| 8 | **Semiotics** | Peirce's sign typology (icon/index/symbol), semiotic channels, limits of symbolic reference, indexicality | "Signs represent things" |
| 9 | **Mathematics of expressiveness** | Descriptive set theory, definability hierarchies (arithmetic/analytic), Beth definability, Craig interpolation | No count unless formal definability concepts are used |
| 10 | **Cognitive science** | Concepts without language, pre-linguistic thought, animal cognition, cognitive universals, conceptual primitives | "Thought exists without language" |

**Maximum M1 score for T4: 10**
