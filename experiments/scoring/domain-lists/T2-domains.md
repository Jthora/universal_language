# Domain List for T2

**Task T2:** "How might the principles of quantum mechanics inform our understanding of belief formation?"

## Pre-Specified Domains for M1 Scoring

| # | Domain | Examples of Substantive Engagement | Examples That Do NOT Count |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Quantum mechanics (standard)** | Schrödinger equation, Born rule, measurement postulate, commutation relations, eigenstate decomposition | "Quantum mechanics is probabilistic" |
| 2 | **Bayesian epistemology** | Prior/posterior distributions, Bayes' theorem, belief updating, credence functions, Dutch book arguments | "Beliefs get updated when we learn new things" |
| 3 | **Cognitive psychology** | Heuristics, biases, dual-process theory, framing effects, anchoring, confirmation bias | "People are sometimes irrational" |
| 4 | **Quantum probability / quantum cognition** | Non-commutative probability, order effects in judgments, conjunction fallacy as quantum interference, Hilbert space models of belief | Merely juxtaposing "quantum" and "belief" |
| 5 | **Philosophy of science** | Theory-ladenness of observation, paradigm shifts, underdetermination, falsifiability, realism vs. instrumentalism | "Science is about testing hypotheses" |
| 6 | **Decision theory** | Expected utility, prospect theory, risk aversion, rational choice, game theory, mechanism design | "People make decisions" |
| 7 | **Neuroscience** | Neural encoding of probabilities, predictive coding, Bayesian brain hypothesis, synaptic plasticity, neural oscillations | "The brain processes beliefs" |
| 8 | **Information theory** | Shannon entropy of belief states, KL divergence as belief distance, mutual information between evidence and belief | "Beliefs contain information" |
| 9 | **Social epistemology** | Collective belief formation, epistemic networks, group polarization, social learning, echo chambers | "Society influences beliefs" |
| 10 | **Formal logic / doxastic logic** | Possible worlds, accessibility relations, KD45, belief revision (AGM theory), conditional logic | "Logic formalizes reasoning" |

**Maximum M1 score for T2: 10**
