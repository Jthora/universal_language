# Task 0.9 — Reframe Experiments

**Phase:** 0 (Honesty Pass)  
**Files:** `frontier/causal-efficacy-protocol.md`, `experiments/README.md`, experiment prompts and scoring docs  
**Effort:** 1–2 days  
**Dependencies:** None

---

## The Problem

The experimental framework is currently positioned as "Proof 4 of Universal Language" — the fourth leg of a four-proof validation framework:

1. Uniqueness (formal)
2. Natural emergence (formal)
3. Generative power (formal)
4. **Causal efficacy on information-processing systems** (empirical)

The issue: the experiments test whether a SPECIFIC ARTIFACT (a compact UL-structured text) causally affects LLM outputs. They do NOT test whether UL's mathematical framework is correct. These are independent claims:

- **Claim A:** UL is a formally valid algebraic system (tested by proofs)
- **Claim B:** UL-structured artifacts produce measurable effects on LLMs (tested by experiments)
- **Claim C:** B validates A (INVALID INFERENCE — B could hold even if A has gaps, and A could hold even if B fails)

## What to Change

### 1. Rename the framing

**Current:** "Proof 4: Causal Efficacy"  
**Replace with:** "Empirical Study: Artifact Effects on Language Models"

The word "Proof" should not appear in the experimental framing. Experiments produce evidence, not proof.

### 2. Separate the claims

Add a section to the protocol document:

```markdown
## Relationship to UL Theory

This experimental protocol tests a specific empirical hypothesis:

> **H₁:** Exposure to UL-structured text causes measurable changes
> in LLM output quality as measured by M1–M5 metrics.

This hypothesis is INDEPENDENT of UL's mathematical validity:
- If H₁ is confirmed, it shows the artifact has causal effects.
  It does NOT prove UL's theorems are correct.
- If H₁ is rejected, it shows the artifact lacks measurable effects.
  It does NOT invalidate UL's theorems.

The connection to UL theory: IF UL is mathematically valid (Proofs 1–3)
AND UL-structured artifacts have causal effects (this study), THEN the
theoretical framework has both formal grounding and empirical relevance.
But neither result validates the other.
```

### 3. Update the protocol document header

**Current header references** to "four-proof framework" and "Proof 4":
- Replace with: "Empirical arm of the UL research program"
- Remove: Any text implying experimental success would complete a mathematical proof

### 4. Update experiments/README.md

Ensure the README:
- Describes what the experiments actually test (artifact effects)
- Does NOT claim the experiments validate UL's formal system
- Includes the separation of claims from item 2 above

### 5. Audit scoring rubrics

Check M1–M5 scoring rubrics in `experiments/scoring/`:
- If any rubric metric is defined in terms of "UL validity" or "proof of UL," reframe in terms of "structural quality of output" or "cross-domain integration"
- The metrics should measure output properties, not UL theory properties

## What to Keep

- The experimental design itself (pre-registered, blinded, controlled)
- The M1–M5 metrics (they measure real output properties)
- The statistical analysis plan
- The test variants and controls
- The infrastructure (run_trials.py, analysis.py, blind.py)

The experiments are well-designed. The problem is purely in the framing — what the experiments claim to show, not how they're run.
